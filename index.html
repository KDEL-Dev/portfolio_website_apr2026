ABOUT ME

My name is Kadelle Liburd, and I am a Web Development student at Humber College, with an expected graduation date of May 2026. I also hold diplomas in 3D Animation from Humber College and Visual Effects for Film and Television from Seneca College.
Prior to transitioning into web development, I worked for over 12 years as a VFX compositor, contributing to a range of television and film projects, including "Blue's Clues & You". This experience helped me develop a strong eye for detail, problem-solving skills, and a solid understanding of visual design.
I am now focused on building a career in front-end or full-stack development. I am most comfortable working with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP, and I enjoy creating clean, responsive, and user-focused web experiences. I am eager to combine my creative background with my technical skills to develop engaging and effective digital solutions.

PROJECTS

Ascent Pokemon Draft League

Ascent is a purpose-built, full-stack platform designed to automate the logistics of competitive Pokémon draft leagues. Developed to replace manual spreadsheets, the application centralizes drafting, roster management, and live stat tracking into a streamlined, data-driven user experience.

My Scale Book

My Scale Book is a digital learning tool built to help musicians master music theory. The app allows users to select a key and instantly see the correct notes for major and minor scales, while also providing high-quality audio previews to help them learn by ear.

D'oh Major - Useless Web

This project was created for a “Useless Web” assignment focused on creativity and skill development rather than practicality. Built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, it's an ear training website that replaces traditional piano notes with Homer Simpson's iconic “D'oh” for a fun, unconventional twist.
